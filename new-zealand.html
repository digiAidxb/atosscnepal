Why Study in New Zealand?

New Zealand is known for its high-quality education system, strong student protection policies, and a balanced lifestyle that supports academic and personal growth.

Globally Recognised Education

Universities and institutes in New Zealand are internationally recognised and focus on practical, research-based learning.

Safe & Student Friendly

New Zealand consistently ranks as one of the safest countries, making it a preferred choice for students and parents.

Clear Visa Process

Student visa requirements are transparent and assessed based on genuine academic and financial readiness.

Is New Zealand the Right Choice for You?

New Zealand is suitable for students who value quality education, safety, and long-term career development over short-term decisions.

+2 / Year 12 Graduates

Students planning undergraduate study in a structured and supportive education system.

Bachelor Graduates

Ideal for students aiming for specialised postgraduate or master’s programs aligned with career goals.

Safety-Focused Students

Students and parents prioritising a calm, secure environment with strong student welfare.

English Language & Academic Requirements

Most New Zealand institutions require proof of English proficiency and relevant academic background. Requirements vary by course and institution.

English Language Tests

IELTS Academic and PTE Academic are widely accepted. Some students may begin the process without test scores.

Academic Background

Academic performance, gaps, and previous study history are assessed case by case.

Preparation Support

ATOSSC Nepal provides IELTS and PTE preparation support, both online and through physical classes.

Cost & Financial Preparation

Students must demonstrate sufficient funds to cover tuition fees, living expenses, and other study-related costs as per immigration guidelines.

Tuition Fees

Tuition fees depend on the course level and institution selected.

Living Expenses

Students must meet the minimum living cost requirements set by Immigration New Zealand.

Source of Funds

Funds may be shown through savings, sponsors, or education loans from approved banks.

Work Rights & Post-Study Opportunities

  • Part-time work during study
  • Full-time work during scheduled breaks
  • Post-study work visa options depending on qualification

Post-study opportunities depend on qualification level, location, and policy at the time of application.
