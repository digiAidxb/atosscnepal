Why Study in the United States?

The United States offers the world’s most flexible and diverse education system, with thousands of institutions and programs to suit different academic and career goals.

Flexible Education System

Students can choose majors, electives, and pathways that match their interests and long-term career plans.

Wide Range of Institutions

From community colleges to top-ranked universities, the USA offers options for various academic profiles.

Global Career Exposure

Studying in the USA provides exposure to international research, innovation, and professional networks.

Is the USA the Right Choice for You?

The USA is suitable for students who want flexibility, diverse learning environments, and strong global exposure.

+2 / Year 12 Graduates

Students interested in undergraduate programs with flexible course structures and credit transfer options.

Bachelor Graduates

Ideal for students aiming for research-based or professional master’s programs.

Profile-Specific Applicants

Students whose profiles benefit from customised university and course selection.

English Language & Academic Requirements

US institutions assess academic performance and English proficiency based on university and program requirements.

English Language Tests

IELTS Academic, TOEFL iBT, and other accepted tests may be required depending on the institution.

Academic Background

GPA, subject relevance, academic consistency, and study gaps are carefully reviewed.

Test Preparation Support

ATOSSC Nepal provides guidance for English tests and academic documentation preparation.

Cost & Financial Preparation

Studying in the USA requires careful financial planning and clear documentation to meet visa and university requirements.

Tuition Fees

Tuition fees vary widely depending on the institution, program, and type of university.

Living Expenses

Living costs depend on the state, city, and lifestyle of the student.

Source of Funds

Funds must be shown through verified savings, sponsors, or approved education loans.

Work Rights & Practical Training

Work opportunities for international students in the USA are regulated and linked to academic programs.

On-Campus Work

Students may work limited hours on campus during their studies.

CPT & OPT

Eligible students may apply for Curricular Practical Training (CPT) or Optional Practical Training (OPT) based on program rules.

Policy-Based Permissions

Work and training opportunities depend on immigration policies at the time of application.
